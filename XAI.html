Explaining GNNs

I have read about multiple ways of people attempted to explain GNNS. These are:

  • Instance based vs Model based: Instance based method aim to uncover the rationale behind a specific prediction made by the model for a given input. Model based methods aim to uncover the general rationale learnt by the model for each posiible output
  • Feature Attribution vs Prototype and Concept attribution: Feature attribution methods aim to assign importance scores between 0 and 1 to each discrete feature of the input. For Graphs these are generally nodes and edges. Concept attribution methods assign importance scores to high level embeddings representative of a learnt concept. In case of graphs these are typically prototypical motifs
  • Post-Hoc vs Ante Hoc methods:Post-Hoc methods aims to explain a trained GNN model where as Ante-Hoc methods restrict information flow during the mofdel's training process
